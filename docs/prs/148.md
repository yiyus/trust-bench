# PR notes: Issue #148 - Reduce the slow test suite to a minute or two

## Summary

Cuts the full, unfiltered test suite (`pytest -q`) from 30-55 minutes to
about 1 minute 22 seconds, meeting the issue's "one to two minutes"
acceptance criterion, via four independent changes:

1. Removes `tests/backends_ext/apl/test_harness.py` (19 tests, each
   spawning a fresh `dyalogscript` process via the one-shot
   `run_harness.sh` path that #139 replaced in production code).
   `run_harness.sh`/`run.dyalog`/`_sources.sh` are untouched, kept as an
   unused-by-tests manual debugging path.
2. Trims `tests/studies/test_dimensionality.py` off n=1000: sweep
   coverage and matrix-free convergence are checked at n up to 200, and
   the dense-vs-matrix-free per-step cost comparison uses n=300 instead
   of n=1000 (still shows the effect; a large margin over the
   comparison's own 3x threshold). `test_dimensionality_apl.py` needed
   no change - it already only solved at n=10.
3. Replaces `test_cli.py`'s real, full-report-generation slow tests with
   a small, fast real smoke test (2 cheap studies) plus a fixture-based
   test that exercises `build_html_report`'s full assembly logic against
   a committed round of real report output
   (`tests/fixtures/report/`, copied from the already-approved
   `docs/prs/135/report/` snapshot), with no solving at all. The
   scalar_cost/trust-apl coverage-gap regression test now selects only
   the two studies the bug concerns, instead of running most of the
   report.
4. Fixes `tests/reporting/test_cross_study.py`'s two `frontier_panels()`
   tests, which turned out to be the single largest cost in the whole
   suite (~218s of the ~270s the `slow` bucket took): `frontier_panels()`
   solves BFGS (a dense method) at n=1000 for its dimensionality panel by
   default, the same n=1000 cost as `test_dimensionality.py`'s, reached
   through a different code path. Fixed by monkeypatching
   `dimensionality.sweep` to a small n within the two tests, with no
   change to `cross_study.py`'s or `dimensionality.py`'s own production
   behaviour - a real report still gets the full n=1000 panel.

This isn't a traditional red/green cycle: there's no new production
behaviour being driven out by failing tests, only the test suite's own
structure and coverage strategy changing. Each change was verified by
running the specific file(s) touched, never the full suite, until a
single final run at the end.

The initial cycle (changes 1-3) reached ~5.5 minutes, short of the issue's
own "one to two minutes" acceptance criterion; a review flagged this gap
explicitly, and the fourth change closed it.

## Cycle 3: CI failure fix

CI failed on `test_dense_hessian_methods_cost_far_more_per_step_at_large_dimension`
at n=200: `assert 0.00602 > (3 * 0.00240)` - a genuine failure, not
flakiness in the usual sense. At n=200 the reference call is fast enough
(single-digit milliseconds) that fixed per-call overhead dominates its own
wall time rather than the O(n)-vs-O(n^2)/O(n^3) cost difference this test
measures; a local calibration (10 repeated trials) confirmed a minimum
observed ratio as low as ~2.5x at n=200, below the test's own 3x
threshold, versus a ~7.8x minimum at n=300. `_LARGEST_N` raised from 200
to 300 (still far below the study's own n=1000, and still ~8-9s for the
whole file across three repeated local runs).

## Non-goals (per the issue)

- `run_harness.sh`/`run.dyalog`/`_sources.sh` are not removed.
- The other ~10 `slow`-marked APL test files (`test_apl_parity.py`,
  `test_apl_bfgs_newton.py`, `test_capability_matrix_apl.py`, etc.) are
  untouched - they exercise real APL solves across many parametrised
  cases and are outside this issue's stated scope. With change 4 applied,
  their combined cost is small enough that the full suite already meets
  the one-to-two-minute target without touching them.

## Implementation

- `tests/backends_ext/apl/test_harness.py`: deleted.
- `tests/studies/test_dimensionality.py`: `_LARGEST_N` lowered from 1000
  to 200; `test_sweep_covers_every_n_method_and_backend` renamed to
  `test_sweep_covers_every_registered_dimension_method_and_backend` and
  now sweeps `N_VALUES` minus 1000; `test_n_values_includes_a_large_dimension`
  added as a no-solve check that `N_VALUES` itself still goes up to
  1000; `test_matrix_free_methods_converge_precisely_at_every_dimension`
  likewise sweeps `N_VALUES` minus 1000; `test_dense_hessian_methods_cost_far_more_per_step_at_large_dimension`
  no longer marked `slow` (now ~5s for the whole file).
- `tests/fixtures/report/` (new): the 22 CSV/PNG files from
  `docs/prs/135/report/` (excluding `report.html`), a real two-backend
  report snapshot already reviewed and approved in #135.
  `scalar_cost.csv` is correctly absent (trust-apl's known coverage gap
  causes it to be skipped when both backends are selected).
- `tests/integration/test_cli.py`: `test_report_command_produces_every_milestone_artefact`
  replaced by `test_report_command_runs_end_to_end_through_the_real_cli_entry_point`
  (real, `only=["baseline", "typical"]`, no longer `slow`) and
  `test_build_html_report_renders_every_milestone_artefact_from_a_committed_fixture_set`
  (fixture-based, no solving, asserts every study present in the fixture
  directory renders its descriptive title in the HTML).
  `test_run_report_with_trust_apl_and_scipy_does_not_crash_on_scalar_costs_known_gap`
  now selects `only=["scalar_cost", "baseline"]` instead of
  `skip_slow=True, skip=["parity_scatter"]` (which ran most of the
  report).
- `tests/reporting/test_cross_study.py`: new `small_dimensionality_panel`
  fixture monkeypatches `cross_study.dimensionality.sweep` to force
  `n_values=[10]` for the duration of the two `frontier_panels()` tests;
  both lose their `@pytest.mark.slow` marker (no longer applicable at
  ~1-2s each).
