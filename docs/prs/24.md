# PR notes: Issue #24 - Study: ill-conditioning

## Summary

Adds the ill-conditioning study (`docs/plans/trust-bench.md`, Section 9
item 3): sweeps the condition number of `trust_bench.problems.families.
ill_conditioned.make(kappa)`'s linear system, recording success (status)
and precision (`dist_to_opt`) for three methods chosen to represent the
three families the issue names: `trust-exact` (exact trust-region),
`Newton-CG` (CG/Krylov), and `BFGS` (dense quasi-Newton). Part of Epic
#21 (Phase 6).

`test_all_three_method_families_converge_precisely_at_moderate_conditioning`
is not a direct reading of either criterion; it establishes the baseline
the other two tests contrast against, showing the three methods only
diverge in behaviour once conditioning is extreme, not from an unrelated
bug at ordinary condition numbers.

`test_sweep_skips_a_method_a_backend_does_not_support` exercises
`sweep()`'s own capability check (a `_BFGSOnlyBackend` test double
declaring only one of the three methods), not named in either criterion,
added because the branch is real, easily and meaningfully testable
(unlike the `large_residual` study's accepted `e > cap` gap, which
genuinely wasn't reachable), and its absence would otherwise be a silent
100%-vs-not-quite coverage gap.

**Design notes**

`KAPPAS = [1, 10, 1e2, ..., 1e8]`. Verified directly against
`SciPyBackend`, all three methods, across the full sweep, before writing
the assertions:

- `trust-exact`'s `dist_to_opt` grows from `0.0` at `kappa=1` to `2.57` at
  `kappa=1e8`, monotonically at every step checked, and its `status`
  flips to `FAILED` from `kappa=1e6` onward: an exact subproblem solve
  loses precision as conditioning worsens, rather than needing more
  iterations (its iteration count stays ~3 throughout the sweep).
- `Newton-CG` and `BFGS` both stay `CONVERGED` across the entire sweep,
  including `kappa=1e8`, where `trust-exact` has already failed.
  `Newton-CG`'s `dist_to_opt` is not monotonic in `kappa` (it dips to
  exactly `0.0` at `kappa=1e4`), so no monotonicity claim is made for it;
  only the empirically-confirmed pass/fail contrast at `kappa=1e8` is
  asserted.
- This problem family is 2-dimensional and exactly linear (quadratic
  cost), which is why `BFGS` performs unusually well here (a well-known
  property: BFGS with an exact line search terminates in at most `n`
  steps on an exact quadratic, regardless of conditioning) rather than
  showing the conditioning sensitivity a general nonlinear problem might.
  The distinguishing signal this study relies on is `trust-exact`'s
  precision failure at extreme conditioning, not a claim that `BFGS`
  degrades with it.

## GREEN cycle (implementation)

**Files changed**

- `trust_bench/studies/ill_conditioning.py` (new): `sweep(kappas=KAPPAS,
  methods=METHODS, backends=BACKENDS)`, keyed by `(kappa, method,
  backend_name)`. Skips a `(method, backend)` pair the backend does not
  declare in its `capabilities()`, rather than letting `Backend.solve`
  raise its own "no method" `ValueError`, so a future backend missing
  one of the three methods degrades gracefully instead of crashing the
  whole sweep.
