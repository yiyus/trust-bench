# PR notes: Issue #110 - Consider a general capability note for trust-exact's indefinite-Hessian fragility

## Summary

#110's own acceptance criteria assumed `ill_conditioning`'s high-kappa
`trust-exact` stall and the typical study's `noisy_expdec`/
`gaussian_peak` `MAX_ITER` failures are "instances of the same
underlying mechanism". Checked directly (Hessian eigenvalues, not
inferred from solver behaviour):

- `ill_conditioning`'s Hessian is exactly `a.T@a` - constant, always
  positive-definite regardless of `kappa` (measured: minimum eigenvalue
  `1.0` even at `kappa=1e7`, condition number `1e14`). Its `trust-exact`/
  `BFGS` fragility is numerical precision loss under an extreme
  condition number, not Hessian indefiniteness.
- `dimensionality` (generalised Rosenbrock) and the typical study's
  `noisy_expdec`/`gaussian_peak` genuinely do have an indefinite
  Hessian away from their zero- or near-zero-residual optimum (measured
  minimum eigenvalues: `-194` at `dimensionality`'s midpoint, `-82`/`-8`
  at the typical study's own standard starts) - but `trust-exact`
  converges through `dimensionality`'s mild indefiniteness reliably
  (through `n=1000`) and only fails on the typical study's more severe
  case.

So the premise was wrong: these are two different mechanisms that
happen to produce a superficially similar symptom (`trust-exact`
failing at an extreme parameter value). #95's own "Status
classification" section in `docs/methodology.md` had already
(incorrectly) attributed `ill_conditioning`'s fragility to Hessian
indefiniteness, citing "large `n` in `dimensionality`" as a co-example -
`dimensionality`'s own trust-exact never actually stalls or fails at
all (only its `BFGS` does, via ordinary `MAX_ITER`, unrelated to the
stall-classification story #95 was about).

This PR corrects that inaccuracy and adds a separate, correctly-scoped
"Capability boundary: trust-exact's indefinite-Hessian fragility"
section, framing `dimensionality` and the typical study as the same
mechanism at different severities (mild-and-harmless vs
severe-and-fatal), explicitly distinct from `ill_conditioning`'s
unrelated numerical-precision boundary.

## Test surface

No bug is being fixed, so there is no RED stage in the usual sense:
three new characterisation tests lock in the Hessian eigenvalue-sign
distinction the corrected documentation claims, protecting against
silent drift, but all three already pass against the current, unchanged
problem definitions (a genuine coverage gap per the issue's own
acceptance criteria, not a red/green pair).

- `tests/problems/test_families.py::
  test_ill_conditioned_hessian_stays_positive_definite_across_the_conditioning_sweep`:
  minimum Hessian eigenvalue stays positive for `kappa` from `1` to
  `1e8`, at the origin, the optimum, and both probe points.
- `tests/problems/test_families.py::
  test_dimensionality_hessian_is_indefinite_partway_between_the_start_and_the_optimum`:
  minimum Hessian eigenvalue is negative at the midpoint between
  `dimensionality(n=10)`'s standard start and its optimum.
- `tests/problems/test_typical.py::
  test_hessian_indefiniteness_at_the_standard_start_matches_trust_exacts_known_fragility`:
  minimum Hessian eigenvalue is negative at the standard start for
  `noisy_expdec`/`gaussian_peak`, positive for `logistic`/
  `michaelis_menten`.

Verified: `tests/problems/` - 65 passed. `ruff check .`: clean.

## Documentation changes

`docs/methodology.md`:

- "Status classification: CONVERGED vs STALLED" - the paragraph
  attributing `ill_conditioning`/`dimensionality`'s STALLED-fix story to
  Hessian indefiniteness is corrected: `ill_conditioning`'s fragility is
  numerical precision loss solving the damped-Newton system at an
  extreme condition number, not indefiniteness; `dimensionality` is
  dropped from this paragraph entirely, since it was never actually part
  of the STALLED-reclassification story (its own fragility, covered in
  the BFGS section, is an ordinary `MAX_ITER`, unrelated).
- "Capability boundary: trust-apl's BFGS under ill-conditioning and
  scale" - a one-sentence note added clarifying this is the
  numerical-precision boundary, not the indefinite-Hessian one.
- New "Capability boundary: trust-exact's indefinite-Hessian fragility"
  section, replacing the old "out of scope" note, covering
  `dimensionality` and the typical study as the same mechanism at
  different severities, and explicitly distinguishing it from
  `ill_conditioning`'s different mechanism.
