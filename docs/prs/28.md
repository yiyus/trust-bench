# PR notes: Issue #28 - Study: dimensionality

## Summary

Adds the dimensionality study (`docs/plans/trust-bench.md`, Section 9
item 7): the generalised Rosenbrock swept at n = 2, 10, 100, 1000,
showing dense-Hessian methods (`trust-exact`, `BFGS`) failing to scale
where matrix-free methods (`Newton-CG`, `trust-krylov`, `L-BFGS-B`) do
not. Part of Epic #21 (Phase 6).

**Implementation scope explicitly includes `trust_bench/backends/
scipy_backend.py`:** `trust-krylov`, one of the three matrix-free
methods the acceptance criterion names directly, is not in
`SciPyBackend`'s method set. Issue #17 deliberately excluded it
("`trust-ncg`/`trust-krylov`... are left out: no acceptance criterion or
plan section currently asks for them"); this issue's acceptance
criterion is exactly that condition being met. Verified directly against
scipy that `trust-krylov` behaves identically in every dimension already
tested for `Newton-CG`/`trust-exact` (requires an analytic Jacobian,
does not support bounds, uses `.jac` not `.grad`), so adding it is a
three-line capabilities addition, not new dispatch logic.

**Design notes**

`N_VALUES = [2, 10, 100, 1000]`, `DENSE_METHODS = ["trust-exact",
"BFGS"]`, `MATRIX_FREE_METHODS = ["Newton-CG", "trust-krylov",
"L-BFGS-B"]`. Verified directly against raw `scipy.optimize.minimize`
across the full sweep before writing any assertions (full benchmark, not
reproduced here in full): at `n=1000` with `max_iter=200`, `trust-exact`
takes `55.985s` and `BFGS` takes `136.165s` (and `BFGS` still fails to
converge, `dist_to_opt ≈ 31.2`), against `L-BFGS-B`'s `4.447s`,
`trust-krylov`'s `13.468s`, and `Newton-CG`'s `24.708s` (all three
converging to `dist_to_opt < 1e-3`).

**Two distinct signals for the "dense methods fail to scale" half of the
claim, chosen deliberately, not redundantly:**

- **Correctness failure (`BFGS`):** at `n=100` alone (fast: `0.947s`),
  `BFGS` already lands at `dist_to_opt ≈ 0.846`, clearly wrong, having
  exhausted its full `200`-iteration budget without converging. This is
  a robust, machine-independent signal: a dense quasi-Newton method
  running out of budget before making real progress, not a timing
  artefact.
- **Per-step cost (`trust-exact`):** `trust-exact` stays *accurate* even
  at `n=1000` (`dist_to_opt ≈ 5e-7`), so its failure to scale shows up
  only in wall-clock time, not correctness. `test_dense_hessian_methods_
  cost_far_more_per_step_at_large_dimension` measures this directly with
  a *ratio* against `L-BFGS-B` on the same machine at the same moment
  (`5x`, comfortably below the `16-36x` margins measured locally),
  rather than an absolute-time threshold, which the project's own metric
  taxonomy (Section 6) already treats as environment-dependent and
  unsafe to assert on directly. A `max_iter=2` budget isolates per-step
  cost from iteration count: `trust-exact`'s eigendecomposition-based
  subproblem solve dominates its cost per step regardless of how many
  steps are taken (verified: `3.638s` for 2 steps at `n=1000`, barely
  less than its 26-step full run's `55.985s` is proportionally more, but
  still overwhelmingly dominated by per-step cost, not step count).

**Test-runtime cost, disclosed and then mitigated, not just disclosed:**
three of this file's four tests are genuinely expensive
(`test_sweep_covers_every_n_method_and_backend`,
`test_matrix_free_methods_converge_precisely_at_every_dimension`,
`test_dense_hessian_methods_cost_far_more_per_step_at_large_dimension`;
the fourth, `test_bfgs_fails_to_converge_at_large_dimension`, stays fast
at `n=100`). Rather than let that cost land on every `make test` run
from now on, added a `slow` pytest marker (registered in `pyproject.
toml`), marked those three, and changed `Makefile`'s `test` target to
`pytest -m "not slow"`. `make coverage` (and therefore CI, which runs
it) is unfiltered and still exercises them in full; only the
fast-iteration `make test` loop skips them. `make test` stays at its
previous ~10s; the slow trio adds roughly a minute to `make coverage`
only, where it belongs.

## GREEN cycle (implementation)

**Files changed**

- `trust_bench/backends/scipy_backend.py`: `"trust-krylov"` added to
  `_MINIMIZE_USES_HESSIAN` and `_MINIMIZE_TOLERANCE_PARAMS` (mapped to
  `frozenset()`), and a `capabilities()` entry (`bounds=False,
  analytic_hessian=True, derivative_modes=_ANALYTIC_ONLY`, identical in
  shape to `Newton-CG`/`trust-exact`). No new dispatch logic: `_solve_
  minimize` already handles this exact capability shape generically.
- `trust_bench/studies/dimensionality.py` (new): `sweep(n_values=
  N_VALUES, methods=METHODS, max_iter=200, backends=BACKENDS)`, keyed by
  `(n, method, backend_name)`.

- `make test`: 176 passed, 3 deselected (`slow`), ~7s — unchanged from
  before this issue.
- `make lint`: clean.
- `make coverage` (unfiltered, includes the `slow` tests): 99% overall;
  `trust_bench/backends/scipy_backend.py` and `trust_bench/studies/
  dimensionality.py` both at 100%. Full run took ~64s wall-clock,
  confirming the disclosed cost lands only there, not in `make test`.
